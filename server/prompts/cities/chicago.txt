Chicago, Illinois, USA — The Windy City on the southwestern shore of Lake Michigan. Architecturally defined by massive steel skyscrapers, iron trunnion bascule drawbridges, grid urbanism, and extensive industrial rail networks.

SONIC IDENTITY: The defining acoustic landscape of Chicago synthesizes natural lake dynamics with heavy industrial transport infrastructure. High-altitude wind tunnels whistling through the skyscraper canyons intersect with the rhythmic clatter of the elevated L-train tracks, metallic bridge iron groans over the Chicago River, and deep maritime foghorns sounding across Lake Michigan.

ARCHITECTURAL & INDUSTRIAL RESONANCE: Massive steel beam structures, caissons, and glass curtain walls act as vast acoustic reflectors. Industrial rail bridges and L-train structures produce heavy low-end sub-drones (~55–110 Hz), metallic rail clatter (fmIndex 4-8), and resonant iron ringing. The Chicago River corridor acts as a natural sound canyon where water surface echoes combine with bascule bridge bell signals.

LAKE MICHIGAN & WIND METEOROLOGY: Gales off Lake Michigan produce atmospheric whistling, wind-sheared harmonics, and low-frequency wind shear drones. Lake foghorns radiate deep, long-decaying sub-bass pulses (40–120 Hz) through dense lake mist.

ACOUSTIC ARCHETYPES FOR THIS CITY:
- Lakefront wind, skydeck heights, atmospheric whistling → drone or bell_sacred (high harmonicity, long decay)
- L-Train elevated tracks, iron bridges, heavy rail transport → industrial (fmIndex 4–9, carrierType: sawtooth/square, filterCutoff 2000–4500)
- Chicago Riverwalk, maritime foghorns, harbor buoys → bell_deep (baseFrequency 55–110 Hz, low harmonicity, decay 4–8s)
- Skyscrapers, metallic mirrors, Cloud Gate reflection → glitch or bell_sacred (fmIndex 1–4, high bitDepth precision)
- Industrial districts, stockyards, rail yards → industrial (carrierType: triangle/sawtooth, bitDepth 4–8)
